📌 Significance / Evidence Context

LITESPARK-022 is a landmark trial: the first adjuvant RCC Ph III to demonstrate DFS superiority versus an active IO comparator (Pembrolizumab), not placebo — a higher evidentiary bar. DFS curves separate within 6 months and remain separated even after treatment ends at 12 months, suggesting durable immunologic memory. FDA priority review accepted 2026. This positions Pembro+Belzutifan as a potential new adjuvant SOC for high-risk ccRCC. Mechanistically: Pembrolizumab (PD-1 blockade) + Belzutifan (VHL-HIF-2α-VEGF axis inhibition at source) = dual anti-tumor immunity + anti-angiogenesis. Builds on KEYNOTE-564 (established Pembro SOC) and LITESPARK-005 (Belzu active later-line). OS data maturity is the remaining question before full SOC adoption.

📌 Significance / Evidence Context

RAMPART (ESMO 2025) demonstrates that adjuvant Durvalumab + Tremelimumab (dual-IO: anti-PD-L1 + anti-CTLA-4) significantly improves DFS vs active monitoring (HR 0.65), with benefit concentrated in high-risk patients (HR 0.52) — the intermediate-risk subgroup showed no benefit (HR 1.19), a critical selection signal. RAMPART is the largest adjuvant RCC trial ever (n=790) and the first positive adjuvant trial including non-ccRCC (16%). The high-risk/intermediate-risk heterogeneity (interaction p=0.019) suggests future adjuvant IO should be refined to truly high-risk patients. Arm B (Durvalumab monotherapy) results in 2026 will answer whether anti-CTLA-4 is necessary for the effect. The lack of QoL deterioration is clinically reassuring. Alongside KEYNOTE-564 and LITESPARK-022, RAMPART now adds a third positive adjuvant IO trial, reinforcing that adjuvant IO works in high-risk RCC — but patient selection matters.

📌 Significance / Evidence Context

CheckMate-914 (both Part A and Part B) confirms neither Nivo+Ipi nor Nivo monotherapy improves DFS vs placebo in adjuvant ccRCC. Part A (Nivo+Ipi, HR 0.92, NS, Lancet 2023); Part B (Nivo mono, HR 0.87, NS, JCO 2025). Key differentiators vs KN-564: shorter treatment duration (~5.5 mo vs 1 yr), CM-914 excluded N1 patients in Part A, and possible drug-specific differences. Exploratory N1 subgroup in Part A showed HR 0.54 (benefit trend). Now superseded by LITESPARK-022 (Pembro+Belzu beats Pembro alone), confirming that the future of adjuvant RCC therapy lies in combination strategies, not single-agent IO.

Key Takeaways
①
Anti-PD-1 class appears necessary for adjuvant benefit
KN-564 (Pembro, anti-PD-1) ✓ and LS-022 (Pembro component, anti-PD-1) ✓ both succeed. IMmotion010 (Atezo, anti-PD-L1) ✗ and PROSPER (Nivo perioperative, anti-PD-1 but short/perioperative) ✗ fail. The anti-PD-1 vs anti-PD-L1 distinction and the importance of pure post-operative adjuvant administration (not perioperative) both appear to matter.
②
pT3a Grade restriction: IMmotion010 excluded low-grade pT3a
IMmotion010 required Grade 3–4 for pT3a entry — paradoxically selecting a higher-risk population still failed. This confirms that risk enrichment alone cannot rescue a less effective IO mechanism. The pT3a any-grade inclusion in KN-564/LS-022 is not the reason for their success.
③
Perioperative ≠ Adjuvant (PROSPER lesson)
PROSPER used Nivolumab perioperatively (1 pre-op dose + 9 post-op doses), but achieved RFS HR 0.97 — completely neutral. Key learning: a single pre-op IO dose is insufficient to prime anti-tumor immunity, and the perioperative context (active surgery, wound healing, immunosuppression) may blunt IO efficacy. Post-operative adjuvant remains the validated approach.
④
2026 Clinical Bottom Line
Pembro + Belzutifan (LS-022): emerging new SOC (FDA priority review accepted); pending OS + full approval.
Pembrolizumab alone (KN-564): current approved SOC (DFS + OS both ✓).
Avoid: Atezolizumab, Nivolumab (±Ipi, ±perioperative) — all negative in this setting.
